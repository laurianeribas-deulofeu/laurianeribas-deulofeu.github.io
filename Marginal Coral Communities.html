2012 - TAIKOR project

While doing my second year of master degree intership in Dr. Chaolun Allen Chen's (BRCAS, Taiwan), I got involved in the TAIKOR project to collaborate with scientists from EWHA university (Seoul, South Korea) & KIOST (Busan, South Korea). In this project, we surveyed Jeju's Benthic communities at 5 & 15 m depths. We also sampled coral colonies to document the diversity of zooxanthellae associating with scleractinian corals in Jeju Island. Since the 2012-initial survey, 3 additional sites have been added to the project (2014). Our findings have already led to several publications (e.g.,Denis et al. 2014a, especially on the high coral cover & recruitment rate of Alveopora japonica (Denis et al. 2014b,Denis et al. 2015). Further publications are currently under review, describing the benthic compositions surrounding Jeju Island & investigating the warming of the ocean alongside the loss of seasonnality over the last 40 years (Ribas-Deulofeu et al., under review.
